About

Sarah Wierschem is a Creative Technologies major at Illinois State University studying game design. She is a freshman and a part of Illinois State University's Game Development Club RSO (Registered Student Organization) where she works in the art team. She was also previously a part of the National Art Honor Society at her high school, Oswego East, where she did art related voulenteer work. Also in high school, she was involved in the band program where she was in Wind Ensemble as well as Jazz Band for all four years, afterwards graduating Summa Cum Laude.

Sarah has done oil painting and oil painting comissions for 7 years and continues to do comission art work. Most commonly, paintings consist of animals or landscapes while her smaller drawings are mostly and normally plants or fictional creatures. She has experience with oil paint, acrylic paint, watercolor, basic drawing, graphite, colored pencils, pastels, clay, metal, and photography. She also has some experience with Adobe, Microsoft Office, and HTML and CSS.

Currently, Sarah would like to become a character designer for a small game studio. She enjoys creating monsters, whether in art or writing, to scare, entertain, and make people think. She will be taking several classes in the future for this, including CTK 240 (Sound Design I), CTK 351 (Game Design I), and CTK 380 (Unity and Unreal Game Engines). She plans on taking many more similar classes in future semesters and will most likely study abroad for a semester her sophomore year.
